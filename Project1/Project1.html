Background Information:

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition. (The color words RED, BLUE were to show up in their actual coloring while PURPLE and ORANGE were to show up as the wrong color, but I was unable to get the font classes set appropriately)

  1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?
    • The independent variable is the word condition, being either congruent or incongruent. The dependent variable is the time taken to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists.
  2. What is an appropriate hypothesis for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.
    • As we begin to analyze the data, we will approach with a set of hypotheses a null hypothesis and an alternate hypothesis.
    • null hypothesis: that the word condition has no effect on the response time. Specifically, H0: Mean difference in time taken is 0.
    • alternate hypothesis: HA: The sample means are not equal.
    • Since we have one population and two conditions, we will use a Paried Samples T-Test to uncover any effects the different conditions may have on the population.

  1. Now, perform the statistical test and report your results. What is your confidence level and your critical statistic value? Do you reject the null hypothesis or fail to reject it? Come to a conclusion in terms of the experiment task. Did the results match up with your expectations?
# H0: Mean difference in time taken is 0:
t.test(stroop$Congruent, stroop$Incongruent, mu=0, alt="two.sided", paired = TRUE, conf.level = 0.99)
## 
##  Paired t-test

## t = -8.0207, df = 23, p-value = 4.103e-08

##  -10.752556  -5.177027

## mean of the differences

The P value is less than 0.0001. By conventional criteria, this difference is considered to be extremely statistically significant. Therefore, we can comfortably reject the null and accept the alternative hypothesis that the word condition does affect the time taken to complete the exercise.
